What is the Merge?

The Beacon Chain is a crucial component of Ethereum 2.0, introduced as part of Ethereum's transition from a Proof-of-Work (PoW) to a Proof-of-Stake (PoS) consensus mechanism. It was launched on December 1, 2020, and played a pivotal role in Ethereum's significant upgrade known as "The Merge" which occurred on September 15, 2022.

The primary purpose of the Beacon Chain is to manage the PoS protocol and coordinate the network of Ethereum stakers. Before validators began validating actual Ethereum blocks, the Beacon Chain served as a ledger of accounts that orchestrated this network of stakers. It's important to note that the Beacon Chain doesn't process transactions or handle smart contract interactions; these are functions of the execution layer of Ethereum.

Another significant aspect of the Beacon Chain is its role in setting up sharding for Ethereum. Sharding is expected to further scale the Ethereum network by dividing its responsibilities across multiple chains. Proof-of-Stake, with its registry of approved block producers, provides a reliable framework for implementing sharding, unlike PoW.

The Beacon Chain introduced staking to Ethereum. In staking, ETH is staked to activate validator software, which creates and validates new blocks in the chain. This approach replaced mining, the primary mechanism under PoW, and is seen as more secure and decentralized.
